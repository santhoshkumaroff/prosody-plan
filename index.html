Core Metrics

Seven metrics designed to capture different aspects of reading fluency and expression

What is this metric?

Measures the quality and appropriateness of inter-word silence duration during reading.

Why do we use this metric?

Pausing demonstrates comprehension and natural phrasing. Children who pause appropriately show better text understanding.

Formula

pause_score = 0.60 × threshold + 0.25 × consistency + 0.15 × variation

Example

Input: 10 words, 9 gaps, 7 in optimal range (100-800ms)

Calculation: 0.60 × 78 + 0.25 × 78 + 0.15 × 80

Result: 78/100

Score Interpretation

80-100 Excellent: Natural, comprehension-indicating pauses
60-79 Good: Appropriate pausing with minor issues
0-39 Poor: Pausing patterns suggest reading difficulty

Educational Relevance

Pausing is a primary indicator of reading comprehension. Research shows that skilled readers pause at phrase boundaries and sentence endings.

What is this metric?

Measures the regularity and consistency of speech timing during reading.

Why do we use this metric?

Regular rhythm indicates fluent reading with consistent pacing. Irregular rhythm suggests decoding difficulty.

Formula

cv = stdev(intervals) / mean(intervals)
rhythm_score = stability(cv)  # Piecewise mapping

Example

Input: Intervals [320, 340, 310, 350, 330]ms

Calculation: CV = 15.8 / 330 = 0.048

Result: 100/100 (CV < 0.1)

Score Interpretation

80-100 Excellent: Consistent, fluent pacing
60-79 Good: Generally regular with minor variations
0-39 Poor: Highly irregular pacing

Educational Relevance

Rhythm reflects automaticity in reading. Fluent readers maintain consistent pace; struggling readers show irregular patterns.

What is this metric?

Measures the prominence and emphasis patterns of words during reading.

Why do we use this metric?

Stress patterns indicate understanding of word importance and sentence structure.

Formula

stress = 0.40 × duration + 0.30 × f0 + 0.30 × energy

Example

Input: Duration: 0.75, F0: 0.65, Energy: 0.70

Calculation: 0.40 × 75 + 0.30 × 65 + 0.30 × 70

Result: 71/100

Score Interpretation

80-100 Excellent: Clear word emphasis patterns
60-79 Good: Appropriate stress with minor issues
0-39 Poor: Flat or inappropriate stress

Educational Relevance

Stress indicates word importance understanding. Children who stress important words demonstrate better comprehension.

What is this metric?

Measures F0 (fundamental frequency) variability and range during reading.

Why do we use this metric?

Pitch variation indicates expressiveness and engagement. Monotone reading suggests limited engagement.

Formula

pitch_score = (range_score × 0.5 + var_score × 0.5) × 100

Example

Input: F0 range: 45Hz, Variability: 0.08

Calculation: (90 × 0.5 + 80 × 0.5)

Result: 85/100

Score Interpretation

80-100 Excellent: Expressive pitch variation
60-79 Good: Appropriate pitch patterns
0-39 Poor: Monotone or inappropriate pitch

Educational Relevance

Pitch variation indicates expressiveness and engagement with the text.

What is this metric?

Detects terminal contour patterns (falling, rising, flat) at sentence endings.

Why do we use this metric?

Intonation indicates sentence type understanding. Questions rise, statements fall.

Formula

intonation = (0.40 × contour + 0.30 × range + 0.30 × variance) × confidence

Example

Input: Contour: 60, Range: 50, Variance: 45, Confidence: 0.8

Calculation: (0.40 × 60 + 0.30 × 50 + 0.30 × 45) × 0.8

Result: 42/100

Score Interpretation

80-100 Excellent: Appropriate sentence-level intonation
60-79 Good: Generally correct with minor issues
0-39 Poor: Incorrect or missing intonation patterns

Educational Relevance

Intonation demonstrates understanding of sentence meaning. This is a key indicator of reading comprehension.

What is this metric?

Combines multiple prosodic elements into a single measure of reading expressiveness.

Why do we use this metric?

Expression captures the overall prosodic quality of reading.

Formula

expression = 0.35 × Intonation + 0.30 × Stress + 0.20 × Pause Variation + 0.15 × Rhythm

Example

Input: Intonation: 70, Stress: 65, Pause Variation: 75, Rhythm: 85

Calculation: 0.35 × 70 + 0.30 × 65 + 0.20 × 75 + 0.15 × 85

Result: 71/100

Score Interpretation

80-100 Excellent: Highly expressive reading
60-79 Good: Appropriate expression
0-39 Poor: Flat or inappropriate expression

Educational Relevance

Expression is the ultimate indicator of reading fluency and comprehension.

What is this metric?

Evaluates sustained reading quality across multiple sentences.

Why do we use this metric?

Paragraphs reveal sustained expression patterns and sentence-to-sentence consistency.

Formula

paragraph = 0.30 × Expression + 0.25 × Intonation + 0.20 × Stress + 0.15 × Pause + 0.10 × Rhythm

Example

Input: Expression: 71, Intonation: 57, Stress: 71, Pause: 78, Rhythm: 92

Calculation: 0.30 × 71 + 0.25 × 57 + 0.20 × 71 + 0.15 × 78 + 0.10 × 92

Result: 70/100

Score Interpretation

80-100 Excellent: Sustained, expressive paragraph reading
60-79 Good: Generally expressive with minor issues
0-39 Poor: Significant paragraph reading difficulties

Educational Relevance

Paragraph prosody indicates advanced reading skills and sustained comprehension.
